Hedge funds are different amongst investment companies in the following two ways:

1. Leverage: Involves borrowing money that is used to deploy their investment ideas.
2. Shorting: When on investor borrows shares and sells them, hoping to buy them back later at a lower price to make a profit.

Quant Hedge Funds: These firms use advanced mathematical models. algorithms and statistical techniques to guide their investment decisions and build a set of repeatable, automated approaches.

Alpha: A mathematical model that seeks to predict the future price movement of various financial instruments.

Quant Ecosystem: The infrastructure of pipeline through which quantitative signals are created, tested and implemented.
